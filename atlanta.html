Atlanta Urban Heat Island Maps: 2016 VS. 2023

This page shows two satellite-derived land surface temperature (LST) maps for Atlanta — one from 2016 and another from 2023. These maps use color gradients to visualize how different areas absorb and retain heat, helping to illustrate the effects of the urban heat island (UHI) phenomenon. You can click the map layer toggles (top-right corner of the map) to switch between the 2016 and 2023 heat maps. This comparison highlights how heat distribution has changed across the city over time.

The maps use a color gradient to visualize heat intensity. Areas with higher temperatures (typically paved, built-up zones) are shown in deep red, while cooler areas (usually parks, forests, or water bodies) appear in blue.
