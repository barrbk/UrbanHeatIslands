Boston Urban Heat Island Maps: 2015 VS. 2024

This page displays two satellite-derived land surface temperature (LST) maps for Boston — one from 2015 and one from 2024. These maps use color gradients to visualize how various parts of the city absorb and retain heat, offering a clear view of the urban heat island (UHI) effect.

You can click the layer toggles (located in the top-right corner of the map) to compare the 2015 and 2023 heat maps. This allows you to explore how surface temperatures have shifted over time.

The color scale reflects temperature intensity. Deeper reds represent hotter surfaces (usually paved or built-up areas), while cooler blues correspond to lower temperatures often found near vegetation or water bodies.
